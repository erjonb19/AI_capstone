About Sorvex 360

Workforce Risk Intelligence for the Utility Sector — Purdue AI/ML Capstone · Spring 2026 · Project L

Sorvex 360 is an AI-powered candidate screening platform built for utility workforce programs. It predicts 1-year retention, safety incident risk, and 24-month promotion potential using a rule-based synthetic dataset of 10,000 utility workforce profiles across six roles.

Model
HistGradientBoosting classifiers trained on 10,000 synthetic profiles · SHAP explainability · Cohort percentile ranking
SHAP explanations appear when feature importance exceeds the model's significance threshold — not all predictions will display key factors.
Data
Rule-based synthetic dataset · 6 utility roles · Phase 1 pre-hire + Phase 2 training program features
Stack
Python · FastAPI · Google Cloud Run · Gemini 2.5 Flash · Vertex AI · BigQuery · HTML / CSS / JS
Built By
Purdue AI/ML Capstone Project · Group L
Supported SOC Roles
SOC stands for Standard Occupational Classification — a federal system used by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics to categorize workers into standardized job families. These six roles were selected because they represent the core skilled trades driving the utility workforce pipeline: high-voltage infrastructure, power generation, water and gas operations, telecommunications, and metering. Together they cover the most in-demand and hardest-to-fill positions across electric, gas, and water utilities.
49-9051.00
Power Line Installer / Repairer
Installs and repairs electrical power transmission and distribution lines. Works at height on energized lines, requiring strict safety compliance and physical endurance.
49-2022.00
Telecom Equipment Installer
Sets up and maintains telecommunications equipment including switching, routing, and transmission systems. Requires strong technical aptitude and attention to detail.
51-8013.00
Power Plant Operator
Controls and monitors equipment that generates electric power. Manages boilers, turbines, generators, and reactors, with high accountability for safety and reliability.

Operates treatment facilities that process water for consumption or treat wastewater before discharge. Requires regulatory knowledge and careful monitoring of chemical processes.

Controls equipment that purifies and processes natural gas for distribution. Works with high-pressure systems in environments requiring rigorous safety protocols and technical precision.

Meter Reader, Utilities
Reads electric, gas, water, and steam meters to record consumption data. Entry-level utility role emphasizing reliability, route efficiency, and customer-facing professionalism.
What We Predict
📋 Retention Risk
Probability a candidate stays employed for at least 1 year. Low risk = high retention likelihood. Weighted 50% of composite score.
⚠️ Safety Risk
Probability of an OSHA-recordable incident in the first year. Low risk = safer candidate. Weighted 30% of composite score.
📈 Promotion Potential
Probability of promotion within 24 months. Low risk = high promotion likelihood. Weighted 20% of composite score.
Ethical AI Policy
Fairness & Bias Commitment
Age and gender are collected for aggregate diversity reporting and bias auditing only — they are never used as model features. Model features are strictly behavioral, simulation, aptitude, and training performance metrics. SHAP values are audited regularly to confirm no feature leakage or proxy reliance on protected characteristics.
